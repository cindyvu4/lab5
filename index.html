Transportation in Seattle

By Cindy Vu | December 10th, 2019

Seattle has been a growing city and is the second-fastest among 50 most-populous U.S. cities. In 2018, the Seattle population was around 745,000 which estimates around a 2.1% increase between 2017-2018, Vs. the slowest city of Baltimore at a -1.2% change. For six consecutive years, Seattle has been ranked among the 5 fastest growing cities. It is the largest city in Washington, in the Pacific Northwest region, and is the 15th largest city in the U.S. For more information on Seattle's growth, read more here.




Parking in the City

With such a huge population in Seattle, driving downtown can often be difficult. City of Seattle public transportation metro bus and link-rail are great alternatives to navigate throughout the busy city for cheap. For drivers, it is incredibly useful to know where parking is available in advance in order to not spend a lot of time driving around to find parking spaces. This map is meant to help identify parking spaces where vehicles are not permitted to stop or park during specific time periods (as stated on the sign) during peak hours. It also depicts paid parking kiosks that issue a receipt to put on the car's dashboard or window to help identify some options for street parking.



Biking in the City

Biking is also a great way to navigate through the city. There is a big cycling community in Seattle, and the city has been on the top tier of the magazine's list of the Best Bike Cities in America. Seattle ranked first place in 2018! The biking infrastructure has been undergoing to make improvements on safety for both pedestrians and cyclists by speed-limit reductions and planned expansions. For more information on the biking community of Seattle, read more here. The map below displays bike rack locations owned and maintained by the Seattle Department of Transportation (SDOT), and existing bike facilities. Existing facilities include all infrastructure currently on the ground and vary from dedicated off-street trails to shared roadway markings (sharrows). It also displays bicycle-related street and off-street (trail) improvements which consist of markings and hardscape that are intended to improve the movement of bicycles through the city.
